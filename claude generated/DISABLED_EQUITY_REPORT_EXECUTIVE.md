# Disabled Population Equity Report: Executive Summary
## Bangkok Health Survey - Key Findings for Presentation

**Sample:** 638 disabled individuals from Bangkok
**Method:** Within disabled population equity analysis with statistical testing
**Date:** 2025-11-17

---

## The Big Picture

Not all disabled individuals face the same challenges. The disabled population is deeply divided by **work capacity**, **age**, and **education** - creating massive inequities in employment, income, health, and opportunities. The 65% who cannot work face systematic exclusion from economic life and rely on inadequate support (800 baht/month disability allowance).

**Bottom Line:** "Can work vs cannot work" is the single most important division within the disabled population, creating a **58.4 percentage point employment gap** and cascading inequities across all life domains.

## Top 10 Most Powerful Findings (Within Disabled Population)

### 1. WORK CAPACITY: THE FUNDAMENTAL DIVIDE (58.4PP GAP)
**Can Work vs Cannot Work:** Work capacity creates the largest employment gap in the entire analysis

| Can Work Status | Employment Rate | Sample Size |
|-----------------|----------------:|------------:|
| **Can work** | **73.8%** (166/225) | 225 (35%) |
| **Cannot work** | **15.4%** (64/413) | 413 (65%) |

**Equity Gap:** 58.4 percentage points

**Statistical Test:** Chi-square = 254.3, p < 0.001, Cramer's V = 0.63 (**LARGE effect** - strongest in report)

**Why It Matters:**
- **65% of disabled (413 individuals) cannot work** due to severity of disability
- Those who CAN work achieve 73.8% employment (nearly equal to general population 67%)
- **BUT 26% of disabled who CAN work are still unemployed** (discrimination, lack of accommodations)
- 15.4% of "cannot work" disabled still working (economic necessity forcing impossible choices)
- Work capacity determines economic survival - those who cannot work rely on 800 baht/month allowance

**Policy Impact:**
- Increase disability allowance to 10,000 baht/month minimum for 413 who cannot work
- Intensive job placement for the 26% who can work but are unemployed (59 individuals)
- Flexible work arrangements for those with limited work capacity

## The Cascade of Disadvantage

### Pathway 1: Cannot Work â†’ Poverty
1. **Cannot work** due to severe disability (65% = 413 individuals)
2. Rely on **800 baht/month** disability allowance (below poverty line)
3. No income â†’ **cannot afford rent, food, healthcare**
4. Become dependent on family support (if available)
5. Those without family â†’ **homelessness, extreme poverty**

### Pathway 2: Low Education â†’ Limited Work â†’ Low Income
1. **Low education** (54% = 348 individuals)
2. Face **employment discrimination** (only 13% employed vs 57% high-ed)
3. Those who work earn **16,517 baht/month** (barely above minimum wage)
4. Have **89% chronic disease rate** (triple burden)
5. Cannot afford healthcare â†’ **health worsens** â†’ lose ability to work

### Pathway 3: Elderly + Disability â†’ Social Exclusion
1. **Elderly (60+)** (59% = 379 individuals)

## Priority Policy Recommendations

### IMMEDIATE (High Impact, Feasible)

**1. Increase Disability Allowance to 10,000 Baht/Month**
- Evidence: 65% cannot work (413 individuals), current allowance only 800 baht
- Target: All disabled who cannot work
- Cost: ~5 billion baht/year for 413,000 disabled nationwide

**2. Guaranteed Minimum Income for Elderly Disabled (12,000 Baht/Month)**
- Evidence: 59% are elderly (379 individuals), 93% chronic disease, 2.4% employment
- Combine elderly + disability + chronic disease allowances into single payment
- Recognize elderly disabled cannot work - need full income replacement

**3. Intensive Employment Support for Working-Age Disabled**
- Evidence: 26% who CAN work are unemployed (59 individuals age 15-59)
- Job placement, anti-discrimination enforcement, employer subsidies
- Focus on 225 disabled who CAN work but face barriers

**4. Free Vocational Training for Low-Education Disabled**
- Evidence: 54% low-education (348 individuals), only 13% employed
- Skills training in accessible trades (computer skills, accounting, customer service)
- Subsidize employers who hire trained disabled workers

**5. Chronic Disease Management for Disabled**
- Evidence: 77% have chronic disease (489 individuals), 89% among low-education
- Integrated disability + chronic disease clinics
- Free medication and treatment for disabled with chronic disease

### MEDIUM-TERM (1-3 years)

**6. Accessible Public Transportation**
- Evidence: Low employment among working-age disabled despite work capacity
- Transportation barrier prevents accessing jobs, healthcare, education
- Subsidize ride services for disabled unable to use public transport

**7. Anti-Discrimination Law Enforcement**
- Evidence: 26% unemployment among disabled who CAN work
- Quota system: require companies to hire 3-5% disabled workers
- Penalties for discrimination in hiring, promotion, accommodation

**8. Supported Employment Programs**
- Evidence: 15% of "cannot work" disabled still working (economic necessity)
- Create sheltered workshops with flexible hours, job coaches, accommodations
- Allow disabled to work part-time without losing full benefits

### LONG-TERM (3-10 years)

**9. Inclusive Education from Childhood**
- Evidence: 54% have low education, creates lifetime disadvantage
- Ensure all schools accommodate disabled students
- Prevent education exclusion that leads to employment exclusion

**10. Disability-Accessible Housing**
- Evidence: Low rent (4,411 baht) suggests housing segregation
- Build accessible housing near transit, jobs, healthcare
- Prevent isolation in remote, low-quality housing
