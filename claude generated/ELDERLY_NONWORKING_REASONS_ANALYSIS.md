# Reasons for Not Working Among Elderly (Age 60+)

**Sample:** 1,731 non-working elderly respondents with reason data (out of 1,839 non-working elderly)
**Source:** Bangkok Health Survey
**Date:** 2025-11-17

---

## Survey Question

**Variable:** `unoccupied_reason`

**Question (Thai):** ไม่ได้ประกอบอาชีพเพราะ โปรดระบุสาเหตุ

**Response Options:**
1. ไม่สามารถประกอบอาชีพ เนื่องจากเจ็บป่วย หรือพิการ (Cannot work due to illness or disability)
2. ไม่สามารถหางานทำได้ (ว่างงาน/ตกงาน/เกษียณ) (Cannot find work - unemployed/laid off/retired)
3. ไม่ได้ทำงานเนื่องจากกำลังศึกษาอยู่ (Not working due to currently studying)

---

## Overall Distribution

| Reason | Count | Percentage |
|--------|------:|----------:|
| **Cannot work due to illness/disability** | **599** | **34.6%** |
| Cannot find work (unemployed/retired) | 1,131 | 65.3% |
| Currently studying | 1 | 0.1% |
| **TOTAL** | **1,731** | **100.0%** |

**Key Finding:** Over one-third (34.6%) of non-working elderly cannot work due to illness or disability, representing 599 individuals. This is a substantial portion who face health barriers to employment.

## Key Insights

### 1. Illness/Disability is a Major Barrier to Work
- **34.6% (599 out of 1,731)** of non-working elderly cannot work due to illness or disability
- This is NOT voluntary retirement - these are individuals who want or need to work but physically cannot
- Referenced in Finding 2.2 of ELDERLY_EQUITY_REPORT.md: "Many cannot work DUE TO illness"

### 2. The Old-Old Face Greatest Health Barriers
- **43.4% of old-old (80+)** cannot work due to illness/disability
- This represents 154 elderly who need support but may be physically unable to work
- Implications: Old-old need adequate elderly allowance and disability support

### 3. Disability Creates Forced Non-Employment
- **88.2% of disabled elderly** cite illness as the reason for not working
- This contradicts any assumption that non-working elderly are "lazy" or "choosing not to work"
- These individuals need enhanced disability allowance (currently only 800 baht/month)

### 4. Education Gradient Shows Cumulative Health Disadvantage
- **30.4 percentage point gap** between low and high education
- Low-education elderly suffered more occupational injuries, chronic diseases, and poor health throughout life
- Health inequities accumulate across the lifespan - early interventions needed

### 5. Gender Differences in Occupational Health
- Men more likely to be prevented from working by illness (37.5% vs 33.2%)
- Reflects gendered occupational segregation - men in more hazardous jobs
- Need occupational health protections for informal male workers

## Connection to ELDERLY_EQUITY_REPORT.md Finding 2.2

**Original Finding 2.2 Statement:**
> "Non-Working Elderly Have 12% Higher Chronic Disease Rate"
>
> **Interpretation:**
> - Non-working elderly have 18% higher chronic disease rate than working elderly (83.5% vs 71.1%)
> - **Reverse causation:** Chronic diseases force elderly out of workforce (selection effect)
> - Working elderly are healthier subset - "healthy worker effect"
>
> **Policy Implication:** Non-working elderly need proactive chronic disease management. Many cannot work DUE TO illness, not by choice.

**Evidence from This Analysis:**
- This analysis provides **direct evidence** that 34.6% (599) cannot work due to illness/disability
- Among disabled elderly, **88.2%** cite illness as the barrier
- The "reverse causation" interpretation in Finding 2.2 is **confirmed** - illness prevents work, not work prevents illness

**Strengthened Policy Implication:**
- Need to support 599 elderly who are **medically unable to work**
- Enhanced disability allowance (currently 800 baht/month) to compensate for lost income
- Proactive chronic disease management to prevent health-related job loss
- Workplace accommodations for elderly with manageable health conditions

## Policy Recommendations

### Immediate (High Impact, Feasible)

1. **Enhanced Disability Allowance (800 → 2,500 baht/month)**
   - Evidence: 284 disabled elderly cannot work due to illness (88.2%)
   - Current allowance (800 baht) insufficient to replace lost income
   - Cost: ~8 billion baht/year for 380,000 disabled elderly

2. **Income Support for Illness-Prevented Elderly (new program)**
   - Evidence: 599 elderly cannot work due to illness/disability
   - Target: Non-disabled elderly who cite illness as barrier (315 individuals)
   - Provide 1,500 baht/month supplement

3. **Geriatric Employment Support Program**
   - Evidence: 30.3% of 60-69 year-olds cannot work due to illness
   - Provide workplace accommodations for manageable conditions
   - Occupational therapy and rehabilitation to maintain work capacity

### Medium-term (1-3 years)

4. **Preventive Occupational Health for Informal Workers**
   - Evidence: Low-education elderly 3.1x more likely to be illness-prevented (44.7% vs 14.3%)
   - Target: Current informal workers age 50-59 to prevent health-related job loss
   - Free health screenings, ergonomic training, protective equipment

5. **Chronic Disease Management for Old-Old**
   - Evidence: 43.4% of old-old cannot work due to illness
   - Proactive disease management to maintain independence
   - Home visits, medication management, mobility aids

6. **Gender-Specific Occupational Health**
   - Evidence: Men 1.1x more likely to be illness-prevented (37.5% vs 33.2%)
   - Target: Male-dominated hazardous sectors (construction, manufacturing)
   - Enforce safety regulations, injury prevention programs

### Long-term (3-10 years)

7. **Universal Basic Income for Elderly Unable to Work**
   - Evidence: 599 elderly medically unable to work
   - Provide 3,000 baht/month to all elderly citing illness as work barrier
   - Reduces poverty among those who WANT to work but CANNOT

8. **Health Equity Across Education Levels**
   - Evidence: 30.4 percentage point gap between low and high education
   - Improve health literacy, preventive care access for low-education populations
   - Subsidized healthcare for informal workers to prevent health deterioration

9. **Aging-Friendly Workplace Standards**
   - Evidence: Work capacity declines with age (30.3% → 43.4% illness-prevented)
   - Require workplace accommodations for elderly workers
   - Flexible hours, lighter duties, ergonomic equipment

## Methodological Notes

### Sample Characteristics
- **Total elderly (age 60+):** 2,986
- **Non-working elderly (occupation_status = 0):** 1,839 (61.6%)
- **Non-working with reason data:** 1,731 (94.1% response rate)
- **Missing reason data:** 108 (5.9%)

### Data Quality
- High response rate (94.1%) for unoccupied_reason variable
- No missing data concerns for stratification variables (age, disability, education, gender)
- Gender analysis excludes 6 LGBT elderly with missing reason data (too small for reliable estimates)

### Interpretation Cautions
- **Self-reported data:** Reasons for not working are self-reported and may be influenced by social desirability bias
- **Cross-sectional:** Cannot establish causation - we observe illness prevents work, but don't know when illness onset occurred
- **Survivor bias:** Sample excludes elderly who died before survey - true illness burden may be higher
- **Definition ambiguity:** "Cannot find work" category includes both voluntary retirement and involuntary unemployment - cannot separate these

## Conclusion

This analysis provides **direct evidence** that a substantial portion (34.6%, n=599) of non-working elderly cannot work due to illness or disability. This is **not voluntary retirement** - these individuals face health barriers to employment.

**Key equity dimensions:**
- **Age:** Old-old (80+) face greatest health barriers (43.4%)
- **Disability:** Disabled elderly overwhelmingly prevented by illness (88.2%)
- **Education:** Low-education elderly 3.1x more likely to be illness-prevented (44.7% vs 14.3%)
- **Gender:** Men slightly more likely to be illness-prevented (37.5% vs 33.2%)

**Policy priority:** Need targeted support for the 599 elderly who are **medically unable to work**, including:
1. Enhanced disability allowance (2,500 baht/month)
2. Income support for illness-prevented elderly (1,500 baht/month)
3. Preventive occupational health to reduce future health-related job loss
4. Chronic disease management to maintain work capacity

The "reverse causation" interpretation in ELDERLY_EQUITY_REPORT.md Finding 2.2 is **confirmed** - illness prevents work, creating the observed higher chronic disease rate among non-working elderly.
