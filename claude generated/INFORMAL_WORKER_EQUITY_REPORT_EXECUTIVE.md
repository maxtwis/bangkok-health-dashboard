# Informal Worker Equity Report: Executive Summary
## Bangkok Health Survey - Key Findings for Presentation

**Sample:** 1,853 informal workers from Bangkok
**Method:** Part 1 - Comparison with formal workers | Part 2 - Equity within informal workers
**Date:** 2025-11-17

---

## The Big Picture

Informal workers (street vendors, delivery riders, construction workers, domestic workers, online sellers) are Bangkok's **invisible workforce** - earning 43% less than formal workers with virtually zero legal protections. Within this population, massive inequities exist by income, age, and education, creating a **deeply divided informal sector**.

**Bottom Line:** Informal workers face a double crisis - systematic exclusion from formal economy protections (96% have NO benefits) AND severe internal inequities (20,111 baht income gap between rich and poor informal workers).

---

## Population Profile: Who Are Informal Workers?

- **Sample size:** 1,853 informal workers (occupation_type=6)
- **Age:** 46% elderly (60+), 31% middle-aged (45-59) - heavily older population
- **Gender:** 65% female, 35% male - predominantly female
- **Income:** 26% low (<10,500 baht), 44% middle (10,500-15,000), 31% high (>15,000)
- **Education:** 44% low-education, 34% middle, 22% high
- **Work hours:** 63% part-time (<8 hrs), 22% full-time, 14% overwork (>10 hrs)
- **Contracts:** Only 4.2% have employment contracts
- **Benefits:** Only 3.8% have work benefits

**Context:** Informal workers are street vendors, motorcycle taxis, delivery riders, construction laborers, domestic workers, cleaners, online sellers, general day laborers.

## Top 10 Most Powerful Findings (Within Informal Workers)

### 1. INCOME INEQUALITY: 20,111 BAHT GAP (267% DIFFERENCE)
**The Internal Divide:** High-income informal workers earn 20,111 baht more than low-income

| Income Group | Mean Income | Sample Size |
|--------------|------------:|------------:|
| **Low (<10.5k)** | 7,530 baht | 355 (26%) |
| Middle (10.5-15k) | 13,256 baht | 604 (44%) |
| **High (>15k)** | 27,641 baht | 422 (31%) |

**Equity Gap:** 20,111 baht/month (Low vs High)

**Statistical Test:** ANOVA F = 849.8, p < 0.001, EtaÂ² = 0.55 (**LARGE effect**)

**Why It Matters:**
- **Low-income informal workers** earn below poverty line (7,530 baht < 11,000 minimum wage)
- **High-income informal workers** earn middle-class wages (27,641 baht)
- 267% gap shows informal sector is NOT homogeneous
- Low-income = street vendors, cleaners, day laborers
- High-income = skilled trades, online businesses, motorcycle taxi owners

**Policy Impact:**
- Minimum income guarantee (15,000 baht/month) for low-income informal workers (355 individuals)
- Sector-specific minimum rates for common informal jobs

### 2. HEALTHCARE ACCESS: 26PP GAP BY INCOME
**The Healthcare Divide:** Low-income informal workers skip care at 31% vs high-income 5%

| Income Group | Skip Medical Care (Cost) | Sample Size |
|--------------|-------------------------:|------------:|
| **Low** | **31.3%** (111/355) | 355 |
| Middle | 10.8% (65/604) | 604 |
| **High** | **5.2%** (22/422) | 422 |

**Equity Gap:** 26.0 percentage points

**Statistical Test:** Chi-square = 105.4, p < 0.001, Cramer's V = 0.28 (**small-to-medium effect**)

**Why It Matters:**
- **1 in 3 low-income informal workers** skip medical care due to cost
- High-income informal workers skip care at general population rate (5%)
- Zero benefits = no health insurance, must pay out-of-pocket
- Low income + healthcare costs = impossible choice (food vs medicine)

**Policy Impact:**
- Universal health coverage specifically for informal workers
- Eliminate co-pays for low-income informal workers
- Mobile health clinics at markets, construction sites

### 3. CHRONIC DISEASE BY AGE: 54.9PP GAP (NEAR-UNIVERSAL AMONG ELDERLY)

- Elderly informal workers work out of necessity (no pension, inadequate elderly allowance)
- 61% chronic disease rate while working = forced to work despite illness
- Working with chronic disease in informal sector (no sick leave, no health insurance) creates health decline spiral

## The Informal Worker Paradox

### Despite Lower Income:
- **LOWER** food insecurity than formal workers (5.1% vs 13.6%)
- **LOWER** healthcare skipping than formal workers (13.3% vs 18.7%)
- **FEWER** work hours than formal workers (8 vs 9 hours)

### Why?
1. **Age effect:** 46% are elderly with family support (adult children provide food/housing)
2. **Household structure:** Many live with working family members who subsidize income
3. **Part-time work:** 63% work <8 hours (flexibility allows household tasks, reduces stress)
4. **Lower expectations:** Earn less but spend less (simpler lifestyle, no urban consumption pressure)

### But Still Face:
- **43% income penalty** vs formal workers
- **96% have NO benefits** (health insurance, sick leave, pension)
- **Zero legal protections** (contracts, minimum wage, labor law)
- **Massive internal inequities** (20,111 baht income gap within informal sector)

## Priority Policy Recommendations

### IMMEDIATE (High Impact, Feasible)

**1. Minimum Income Guarantee (15,000 Baht/Month)**
- Evidence: 26% earn below poverty line (7,530 baht), 355 low-income workers
- Target: All informal workers earning <10,500 baht/month
- Mechanism: Wage subsidy + conditional cash transfer

**2. Universal Health Coverage for Informal Workers**
- Evidence: 31% of low-income skip care due to cost (111 individuals)
- Eliminate ALL co-pays for informal workers
- Free medication and treatment for chronic disease (44% have chronic disease)

**3. Elderly Informal Worker Support (60+)**
- Evidence: 46% are elderly (846 individuals), earn 10,185 baht, 61% chronic disease
- Enhanced elderly allowance (3,000 baht/month) + informal worker top-up (2,000 baht)
- Total 5,000 baht/month for elderly informal workers

**4. Simplified Social Security Registration**
- Evidence: 96% have NO benefits
- One-stop registration: health insurance + pension + injury compensation
- Government-subsidized premiums for low-income workers

**5. Mobile Health Clinics at Work Sites**
- Evidence: Healthcare access barriers for low-income (31% skip care)
- Clinics at markets, construction sites, transit hubs
- Free check-ups, medications, chronic disease management

### MEDIUM-TERM (1-3 years)

**6. Informal Worker Cooperatives**
- Evidence: Zero bargaining power, income instability
- Establish cooperatives by sector (street vendors, delivery riders, construction)
- Collective bargaining, bulk purchasing, income pooling

**7. Skills Training Programs**
- Evidence: Low-education (44%) earn 45% less than high-education
- Free vocational training in growth sectors
- Transition pathways from informal to formal employment

**8. Work Injury Insurance for Informal Workers**
- Evidence: 5.6% serious injury, 14.4% minor injury
- Subsidized injury insurance (50 baht/month premium)
- Coverage: medical costs + income replacement during recovery

### LONG-TERM (3-10 years)

**9. Formalization Incentives**
- Evidence: 96% have NO contracts
- Tax breaks for employers who formalize informal workers
- Simplified labor contracts (1-page, Thai language)

**10. Pension System for Informal Workers**
- Evidence: 46% elderly working out of necessity (no pension)
- Voluntary savings-matched pension (government matches contributions 1:1)
- Minimum pension guarantee (3,000 baht/month at age 60)

## Key Takeaways for Presentation

### The Story Arc:

1. **The Invisible Workforce:** 1,853 informal workers = street vendors, riders, cleaners, construction workers
2. **The Structural Gap:** 43% lower income, 96% NO benefits, 96% NO contracts
3. **The Internal Divide:** 20,111 baht income gap between richest and poorest informal workers
4. **The Age Crisis:** 46% are elderly working out of necessity (10,185 baht, 61% chronic disease)
5. **The Healthcare Trap:** Low-income informal workers skip care at 31% despite needing most help

### Powerful Statistics:

- **12,306 baht/month** income penalty (vs formal workers)
- **96%** have NO benefits (health insurance, pension, sick leave)
- **20,111 baht** income gap within informal sector
- **31%** of low-income skip medical care
- **46%** are elderly (846 individuals)
- **61%** of elderly have chronic disease while working
- **267%** income inequality (high vs low)

### The "So What?" Factor:

**Informal workers are NOT a homogeneous underclass.**

The informal sector contains:
- **Elderly working to survive** (10,185 baht/month, no pension)
- **Youth gig workers** earning middle-class wages (19,408 baht)
- **Disabled excluded from formal sector** (triple burden)
- **Parents choosing between doctor and children** (21% skip care)

**Current system offers zero protection:**
- No minimum wage
- No health insurance
- No sick leave
- No pension
- No contracts
- No legal recourse

**The solution requires comprehensive social protection:**
1. **Minimum income guarantee** - 15,000 baht/month floor
2. **Universal health coverage** - eliminate co-pays for informal workers
3. **Elderly support** - enhanced allowance for working elderly
4. **Formalization incentives** - pathways from informal to formal work

**The evidence is clear. The crisis is real. The solutions are feasible.**
